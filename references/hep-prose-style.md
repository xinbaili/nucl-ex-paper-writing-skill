# HEP Prose Style

Use this reference when drafting or polishing introductions, abstracts, captions, result interpretations, conference abstracts, and responses where the user wants natural HEP/nucl-ex prose.

## Core Principle

Write concrete physics prose, not conceptual filler. The sentence should usually be carried by an observable, measurement, collision system, physical mechanism, model ingredient, analysis method, or experimental consequence.

Do not use word-level blacklists. Words such as "interplay", "framework", "picture", "connection", "perspective", "window", or "probe" are allowed when they refer to specific physics. Treat them as review triggers, not forbidden words.

Revise the sentence only when an abstract phrase replaces the physics that should be stated directly.

## Context Strictness

- **Journal paper introduction**: Use concise, concrete prose with minimal metadiscourse. Assume a HEP/nuclear-physics reader.
- **Conference abstract**: Broader motivation language is acceptable, but keep each physics relation specific.
- **Thesis or pedagogical note**: Explanatory framing is allowed when the user asks for teaching or background.
- **Figure caption and result text**: Prefer direct statements about what is shown, measured, corrected, compared, or inferred.
- **Reviewer response**: Mirror the reviewer's terminology when useful, then state the concrete manuscript change.

## Concrete-Subject Preference

Prefer sentence subjects such as:

- the measured yield, cross section, ratio, cumulant, spectrum, correlation function, or flow coefficient;
- the collision system, centrality class, multiplicity class, rapidity interval, or pT range;
- coherent photoproduction, recombination, fragmentation, jet quenching, radial flow, baryon-number conservation, volume fluctuations, cold nuclear matter effects, or another named mechanism;
- a pp reference, Glauber calculation, response matrix, background estimate, fit model, or systematic variation;
- a specific model calculation or prior measurement.

Use abstract subjects only when they name established concepts and add information. Avoid making the main sentence subject a vague relation such as "the connection", "this formulation", "this map", "a useful way", or "the nature of" when a concrete physics subject is available.

## Review Test

When polishing a sentence, ask:

1. Does it name a specific observable, mechanism, model ingredient, method, system, kinematic region, or measurable consequence?
2. Would a STAR, ALICE, CMS-HIN, ATLAS-HI, PHENIX, or sPHENIX paper plausibly use this sentence in an introduction or result paragraph?
3. Is it explaining basic QFT, special relativity, quantum mechanics, or probability to a reader who already knows the field?
4. Does an abstract noun hide the actual physics relation?
5. Can the sentence be shortened by making the observable or mechanism the subject?

Rewrite if the answer indicates filler, tutorial prose, or an AI-style bridge. Keep the sentence if the abstract wording is standard and tied to concrete physics.

## Accepted Uses Of Abstract Terms

Good:

- "The interplay between recombination and fragmentation affects baryon-to-meson ratios at intermediate pT."
- "The interplay of cold nuclear matter effects and final-state suppression complicates the interpretation of quarkonium production in pA and AA collisions."
- "Hydrodynamic calculations provide a framework for relating anisotropic flow measurements to the transport properties of the medium."
- "Two-particle correlations provide a window on collective behavior in small collision systems."

These are acceptable because the abstract term points to named mechanisms, observables, or model assumptions.

Weak:

- "The connection between the QCD phase diagram and experiment is made through fluctuations."
- "A useful way to view QFT is that each field carries both spacetime properties and internal quantum numbers."
- "This formulation is necessary because special relativity allows energy to be converted into particle production, while quantum mechanics fixes the probabilistic nature of the transition."

These are weak because they sound pedagogical or replace the specific experimental statement with broad conceptual framing.

## Preferred Sentence Patterns

- Observable or measurement + probes/constrains/quantifies + physics mechanism.
- Collision system or event class + exhibits/shows/modifies + measured trend.
- Model or calculation + describes/underpredicts/overpredicts + data within uncertainties.
- Method or correction + accounts for/removes/estimates + specific background, bias, or detector effect.
- Prior measurement + established/showed/reported + remaining gap.

Useful verbs include:

- probe, constrain, quantify, test, compare, separate;
- suppress, enhance, modify, broaden, shift, scale with, deviate from;
- describe, underpredict, overpredict, reproduce, fail to reproduce;
- account for, correct for, subtract, unfold, normalize, estimate.

Avoid making prose more ornate during polishing. Prefer shorter sentences when a long sentence only adds framing.

## Example Rewrites

Weak:

"The connection between the QCD phase diagram and experiment is made through event-by-event fluctuations."

Better:

"Event-by-event fluctuations of conserved charges provide experimental constraints on the QCD phase structure."

Weak:

"This formulation is necessary in high-energy processes because special relativity allows energy to be converted into particle production, while quantum mechanics fixes the probabilistic nature of the transition."

Better:

"Particle production in high-energy collisions is described using Lorentz-invariant observables and transition probabilities."

Weak:

"This map still contains both coherent and non-coherent contributions, so it should be read as a visual summary of the measured distribution before the statistical coherent subtraction."

Better:

"The distribution includes both coherent and incoherent contributions before the statistical subtraction."

Weak:

"A useful way to view QFT is that each field carries both spacetime properties, such as spin and Lorentz transformation behavior."

Better:

"In quantum field theory, fields are characterized by Lorentz transformation properties, spin, and internal quantum numbers."

## Final Polish Pass

Before returning polished manuscript prose:

- remove sentences that only announce what the text will discuss;
- replace tutorial explanations with the specific definition or experimental consequence needed here;
- keep standard HEP terms when they are attached to concrete physics;
- preserve uncertainty language and do not strengthen the claim;
- do not change collaboration style, observable definitions, or technical caveats.
